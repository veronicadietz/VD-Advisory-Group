The Container i. What it is

One issue. Named, examined, and restructured in focused time.

The Orientation Intensive exists for founders who already know where the pressure is building but can't see the shape of it from inside the business. You don't need a 12-week container and you don't need another strategy session. You need somebody to sit with the problem at a structural level and give it a name.

This is the work between "something is off" and "I know exactly what to restructure."

Most intensives in this industry are glorified coaching days. This isn't that. The work is diagnostic, the output is decision-grade, and the container is built to produce a specific result: one load-bearing issue named, examined from multiple angles, and translated into a clear structural move.

Fit ii. Who it's for

This is the right container for a specific kind of founder.

Right fit

  • You've run the business long enough to sense a structural issue, not just a tactical one
  • You've already tried the obvious fixes and they didn't hold
  • You want focused time on one specific question, not ongoing accountability
  • You trust your own judgment and need a thinking partner, not a coach
  • You're ready to act on the read once the work is done

Not the right fit

  • You're looking for motivation, accountability, or ongoing check-ins
  • You want someone to build or execute the thing, not diagnose it
  • You haven't yet identified which area of the business feels load-bearing
  • You're early enough in the business that structural work is premature
  • You want a done-for-you strategy deliverable without co-diagnosis
The Structure iii. How it moves

Three phases. One named outcome.

The Intensive runs across a focused container of private sessions and asynchronous thinking. The shape is fixed. The specifics are built around the issue you bring in.

Excavation

Before we meet, you submit a structured intake covering the business as it exists today. Not the pitch version. The real one. I read it against the pattern library and come in with hypotheses already forming.

Live Diagnosis

Focused 1:1 time working the issue from multiple angles. Positioning, offer structure, audience fit, decision architecture. We name what's actually load-bearing and test the read against everything downstream.

Decision Map

You leave with a written artifact. Not a slide deck, not a strategy document. A named pattern, the structural move that follows from it, and the decision framework for executing without relitigating.

Questions v. What comes up

The real questions founders ask.

Why isn't the Intensive listed with a buy button?

Because it shouldn't be. The container is shaped around a specific structural issue, and buying it without first naming that issue usually produces a worse outcome than skipping the Intensive entirely. The Direction Session is the filter. It protects both of us.

What if I already know what the issue is?

You might be right. You also might be looking at a symptom. The Direction Session is short enough to confirm the read quickly. If you've named the actual load-bearing issue, we move straight into designing the Intensive. If you've named a surface pattern, we find the structural one together first.

How long is the Intensive itself?

The specifics get designed during the Direction Session based on the issue we're working. What's fixed is the structure: excavation, live diagnosis, decision map. What varies is how those phases get shaped around your business.

Is this a one-time engagement or ongoing?

One-time and contained by design. The Intensive is built to produce one clear structural outcome, not to become a retainer. If ongoing advisory is the right fit, there are containers for that. The Intensive stays what it is.

What if the Direction Session reveals I need something else?

Then you get told that. Sometimes the Intensive is the right move. Sometimes Stop Starting Over is. Sometimes the issue is smaller than it feels and a single Direction Session resolves it. The only way the Direction Session fails is if we don't name the real pattern.

If the pressure in the business is structural, the Direction Session is where it gets named.

From there, the Orientation Intensive is one of several possible next moves. You'll know which one is right before you book it.
